[ Situation ]
Why it matters ?
Root + key = Rootkie

“After the pandemic, the demand for growing plants is increasing, but it is difficult to obtain accurate information, and as a beginner, it is too difficult to manage plants regularly.”



‍

People usually feel a sense of accomplishment with their plants' growth every day and as well, and green area helps to reduce stress.
‍
But they always feel that they lack information about plants. Every time when people search on the Internet, they get confused by the amount of knowledge, so it's easy to overlook the information they need, and it's difficult to give the right amount of water due to complicated terms and hard-to-recognize designs.

[ process ]
We developed by following 4 phases : Discover, Define, Develop and Design

We moved back and forth among the four phases, as the design process is not linear. We sometimes needed to go back and redesigned based on user testing. Finally, we learned more about the topic.

[ 03. Develop ]

[ Develop ]
1. Sketches

Hand sketches provided a quick and tangible way to explore and iterate on design ideas. 3 task flows were identified based on the features of the app



‍

1. Identifying a plant
2. Adding a plant to a personal set
3. Monitoring the added plant with the app status & notifications

We tested the concepts and validated the steps with the help of brainstorming and inviting a few of the past users to comment.

2. Mid-fidelity Wirefrmae

Post laying out the idea of the main features and flow, we dived into positioning the elements into a digital mockup. This made the picture more clear of what additional elements are required to support the page and what can be simplified.



‍
3. User testing

Conducted user testing and iteration



‍

We tried to embed the gamification concept with a progress level indicator and provide relevant and accessible information about potted plants to the user in a friendly gamified manner to increase engagement and increase awareness, but after user testing, nobody understood the function and also it causes the confuse with other features.

[ 04. Deliver ]

4 Key Features

Unlock Your Green Thumb with "Rootkie"

"The Ultimate Planting Companion for the plant lovers"

Prsonalization

For a personalized user experience, user location and their planting expertise level were the main screens of the onboarding stage.

Manage your tasks

The priority of the Home screen was kept on the daily scheduling of tasks with ease of accessing the plants and keeping the information part decluttered.

Search & Add Plants easily

The interface is intuitive and user-friendly with clear sorting and search options. Users can easily find plants based on their preferences and needs. A customized task section and the ability to add a nickname for their plant buddies make the experience more personalized.

Scan & Check the condition

The scanning feature was for identifying different parts and the health of the plant to provide more specific information. It was important for it to be accurate and reliable to build trust with the user.

[ 05. Summary ]

[Project Learning]

1.Crucial nature of user research : Gaining valuable insights about the target audience, their needs, pain points, and expectations from the app
‍
2. Intuitive design and clear navigation: Designing for a beginner audience meant keeping the user experience simple and easy-to-use 
‍
3.Keeping the design visually appealing: As the app was related to plants, color association while retaining the freshness of visual system was important 
‍
4.Providing relevant & factually correct information: Care requirements, growth habits, and any potential issues that could arise
‍
5.Using clear & simple language: Avoiding any technical jargon that a beginner may not understand
‍
 6.Continuous iteration & testing: Ensuring that the app was meeting user needs and expectations

[Future steps]

1. Inclusivity: Create a diverse set of plant recommendations and care tips that cater to the needs of a wide range of users, including those with limited space, those living in odd condtitions, and those with allergies.
‍
2.  Accessibility: Incorporate features that make the app more accessible, such as voice command, screen reader support, and adjustable font sizes.

3.  Expanding the user set: Iterate design and functionality of the app based on user feedback and testing until it meets the needs of advanced users. 

4. Standard guidelines: Ensure that the app is designed with accessibility guidelines and standards in mind, such as those provided by the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)
